[STORY]

The beauty of becoming

This ring was born almost playfully, starting from the form of a realistic beetle that was deliberately deconstructed and transformed into something new. A light yet meaningful creative process that speaks of transformation: as in life, what ends does not disappear, but changes form and continues to exist, revealing unexpected beauty.

[CRAFTING]

Handcrafted using the lost-wax technique. Born from an initial model later transformed, it retains traces of its origin in the open, fragmented structure, evoking at once an eye or a void. The irregular surface, etched with lines that seem to melt away, recalls volcanic matter or a process of decomposition.

The search for balance

The design draws inspiration from the search for both inner and everyday balance. Two opposing ends — one flat, the other inclined — engage in a dialogue between stability and tension. A balance never final, always on the verge of shifting, embodying the constant oscillation that defines life itself.

[CRAFTING]

[STORY]

Strength of stone, sculpted by time

The 'Rock' ring was born from the desire to transform the strength of stone, sculpted by time, into a jewel. Its marked surface speaks of erosion by the elements: one side strong and resistant, the other crossed by fissures that let the light seep through. An imperfect balance that reflects the beauty of nature and the memory it carries.

[CRAFTING]

Ring handcrafted using the lost-wax casting technique. The process creates a textured surface with cavities and reliefs that enhance its sculptural character. The irregular shape and the play of light and shadow make each piece different from the other, celebrating the artisanal process that makes it truly unique.

UNDERTOW RING

  • Sterling Silver 925
  • Lost-wax casting
  • Oxidized surface with polished details
  • Adjustable open ring
Request info

[STORY]

The slow movement of seaweed

This ring was born from the desire to capture the slow movement of seaweed beneath the surface — their silent, vertical sway in a world where time seems suspended. It embodies the deep calm and slowness we often lose today, that fleeting breath we can only sense underwater, in a silence that restores and envelops.

[CRAFTING]

Ring handcrafted using the lost-wax casting technique. The open, adjustable band has been oxidized to emphasize its depth and detail. The design is inspired by seaweed, climbing the finger as if defying gravity, with vertical and irregular forms that evoke the fluid, organic movement of the ocean floor.
